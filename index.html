VALUATION 101

What, When, and How of Getting Compliant

What is a 409A Valuation?

It determines the Fair Market Value (FMV) of your companyâ€™s common stock, required by the IRS under Section 409A. This protects both the startup and employees from penalties by ensuring ESOPs and stock options are priced correctly. It's a key part of financial due diligence.

Read Blogs for details...

When Do You Need It?

  • Every 12 months (must be renewed annually).
  • After a new funding round (equity, SAFE, or convertible note).
  • Upon major business changes (e.g., pivot, acquisition talks).

How is the Valuation Done?

We select the optimal method based on your stage and complexity:

  • OPM: Option Pricing Model (Post-Seed)
  • Backsolve: Post-Financing Rounds
  • DCF & PWERM: Growth/Late Stage

All methods are documented according to IRS, AICPA, and NACVA guidelines.
